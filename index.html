Geo Lee

I am a Combined Honours Computer Science and Mathematics student at The University of British Columbia (UBC) in Vancouver, Canada, interested in music and audio computing, machine learning, and computational approaches to musical creativity.

About

I am interested in research on musical creativity in AI, especially the problem of what a creative music model would require beyond surface-level stylistic imitation. I am particularly drawn to generative models for music and sound, representation learning for musical structure, and questions about how composition, production, and expressive decision-making might be modeled computationally. Outside formal coursework and research, I also produce electronic music independently, which is a major reason I am drawn to music and audio research, especially questions of musical structure, sound design, originality, and what creative AI systems for music might look like.

Experience

My recent experience combines machine learning systems work with audio-related pipelines, real-time experimentation, and research engineering. It has given me practical experience that I want to apply more directly to music and audio research.

Projects

UBC PLAI · 2025

A Comparative Analysis of Embodied AI Agents in Minecraft

Developed a live multiplayer experimental environment for evaluating agent execution beyond simulated benchmarks. Compared symbolic planning and reactive control strategies under real-time system constraints, and designed evaluation protocols measuring robustness, responsiveness, and task performance in a dynamic interactive setting. This project combined experimental system design with practical evaluation of embodied agents under real-world latency and coordination constraints.

Independent project · 2022

Deep Neural Network Based Music Key Detection System

Designed and implemented an end-to-end machine learning pipeline for music key detection from audio recordings, including data collection and preprocessing, feature design, model training, and evaluation. Through this project, I became interested in the relationship between audio representations, prediction confidence, and the role of musical structure in learning-based systems.

Independent project · 2022

A Software Simulation of the Proof of Roth’s Theorem

Implemented a modular software simulation of ideas related to Roth’s theorem using discrete Fourier analysis. The project explored computational perspectives on combinatorial proof structure and mathematical abstraction, and reflected my broader interest in formal reasoning, algorithmic structure, and mathematically grounded computational systems.
